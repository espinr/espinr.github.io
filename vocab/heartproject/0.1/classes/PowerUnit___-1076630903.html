Class: 'Power Units'

Annotations (4)

  • description "In physics, power is the rate at which energy is transferred, used, or transformed. The unit of power is the joule per second (J/s). Energy transfer can be used to do work, so power is also the rate at which this work is performed. The integral of power over time defines the work done. Because this integral depends on the trajectory of the point of application of the force and torque, this calculation of work is said to be "path dependent." The dimension of power is energy divided by time."(rdf:HTML)
